# Test 1 — Questions & Answers

### Q1: Why is behaviourism largely incompatible with the basic assumptions of cognitive science?
- A. Behaviourism is not scientific.
- B. Behaviourists study non-psychological observable phenomena and measurable behaviour, while cognitive science is the study of cognition and mind.
- C. Cognitive scientists are not interested in behaviour.
- D. Behaviourists study non-psychological observable phenomena and measurable behaviour, while cognitive science is the study of association and reinforcement learning.

**✅ Correct: B. Behaviourists study non-psychological observable phenomena and measurable behaviour, while cognitive science is the study of cognition and mind**

### Q2: What is the main difference between classical conditioning and operant conditioning (reinforcement learning)?
- A. Classical conditioning works through reflexive behaviour, while operant conditioning works through agent-initiated behaviour.
- B. The main difference lies in the complexity of the organism; only complex organisms can learn to use reinforcement.
- C. Classical conditioning uses reinforcers, while operant conditioning uses an unconditioned stimulus.
- D. Classical conditioning works through agent-initiated behaviour, while operant conditioning works through reflexive behaviour.

**✅ Correct: A. Classical conditioning works through reflexive behaviour, while operant conditioning works through agent-initiated behaviour**

### Q3: In Pavlov's experiments, before conditioning: the bell was a(n) ______ stimulus, while the food was a(n) ______ stimulus.
- A. neutral; unconditioned
- B. conditioned; unconditioned
- C. unconditioned; neutral
- D. neutral; conditioned

**✅ Correct: A. neutral; unconditioned**

### Q4: Which key idea in the development of cognitive science is attributable to Alan Turing?
