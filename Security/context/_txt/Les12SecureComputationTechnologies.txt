Homomorphism in RSA
Given: c_1 = m_1e mod n and c_2 = m_2e mod n Unpadded RSA allows us to compute: c_1 * c_2 = (m_1e mod n) * (m_2e mod n) = (m_1 * m_2)e mod n , which then decrypts to the product of the two plaintexts: (c_1 * c_2 )d mod n = m_1 * m_2
- Malleability... allows for attacks - But, also.... for Secure Computation schemes

Multiparty Computation Context
· A minimum of two parties (servers) is required to perform computation · Trust is distributed and there are various security models one can consider · Multiple rounds of communication required in an interactive protocol · Communication and computational cost is considerably larger than
plaintext computations (yet more efficient than HE solutions!)
